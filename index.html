Project 1 · Research

Microvascular Resistance and Coronary Diagnostic Indices

BioSiMM Lab, Carnegie Mellon · 2024–present

Poster (PDF)

Objective: quantify how downstream microvascular conditions can shift coronary diagnostic indices across decision thresholds—even when anatomy and lesion severity are held constant—so interpretation is more patient-specific.

Skills highlighted: Coronary CFD · Lumped-parameter networks · HPC automation · ParaView/VTK scripting · Python analysis

Details ↓ ▾

Introduction

Pressure- and flow-derived coronary indices can be sensitive to microvascular state. Improving interpretation can reduce misclassification risk and support better treatment decisions.

Methods

Built 3D coronary models coupled to lumped-parameter networks; executed parameter sweeps over resistance states; post-processed hemodynamics and indices at scale.

Results

Demonstrated that identical lesions can shift across decision thresholds under microvascular-only changes.

Discussion

Motivates microvascular-aware interpretation, especially in borderline cases where thresholds are used rigidly.

Role

Led simulation pipeline execution and post-processing automation; synthesized findings for communication in poster format.

Project 2 · Hemodynamics

Carotid Bifurcation Angle & Stenosis Influence on Hemodynamics

Carotid CFD Study · 2024

Video Report (PDF)

Objective: evaluate how geometric variation (bifurcation angle) and localized stenosis modulate WSS patterns linked to vascular risk, supporting better mechanistic reasoning about disease progression.

Skills highlighted: Vascular CFD · Pulsatile flow · Mesh generation · Boundary conditions · WSS quantification

Details ↓ ▾

Introduction

Regions of abnormally low or high WSS are associated with plaque initiation and destabilization. Understanding geometry-driven changes supports clearer hemodynamic intuition.

Methods

Systematically varied angle and stenosis; ran pulsatile simulations; computed surface-area exposure to low/high WSS at key cardiac phases.

Results

Quantified changes in low-WSS exposure with stenosis/angle adjustments; summarized directional effects of geometry changes.

Discussion

Suggests geometry can amplify or mitigate hemodynamic risk factors, reinforcing the value of controlled parametric studies.

Role

Built models/meshes, ran simulations, and performed WSS analysis; packaged outputs into report + media for review.

Project 3 · Environmental CFD

Decarbonization Building CFD: Reducing Re-Entrained Exhaust

Computational Fluid Dynamics · Fall 2024

Scalar transport (GIF)

Objective: reduce energy waste in direct-air-capture facility concepts by identifying shapes and operating conditions that minimize exhaust re-entrainment into inlets.

Skills highlighted: External-flow CFD · Transport modeling · Parametric studies · Engineering communication

Details ↓ ▾

Introduction

Re-entrainment can lower CO₂ removal efficiency by feeding processed exhaust back into the intake, increasing operating cost and reducing climate impact per unit energy.

Methods

Defined a simplified domain and boundary conditions; tested candidate geometries; tracked a passive scalar as a proxy for processed exhaust.

Results

Identified configurations associated with reduced near-inlet recirculation and lower exhaust fraction at the intake.

Discussion

Provides early-stage design guidance where small changes can materially impact efficiency and operating cost.

Role

Implemented transport diagnostics and contributed to simulation workflow + synthesis of results into a structured write-up.

Project 4 · Deep Learning

Modular Diffusion Model Framework for Image Synthesis (Built From Scratch)

Deep Learning · Fall 2024

Report (PDF)

Objective: implement a complete diffusion-based generative modeling stack from the ground up and quantify how architectural and algorithmic choices shift the quality–compute tradeoff in training and sampling.

Skills highlighted: PyTorch · DDPM/DDIM · Latent diffusion · Classifier-free guidance · EMA/schedules · Evaluation (FID/IS)

Details ↓ ▾

Introduction

Diffusion models are stable and high-quality, but can be expensive in pixel space. This project targets practical efficiency improvements while preserving fidelity.

Methods

Built baseline DDPM; added DDIM for faster deterministic sampling; implemented latent diffusion with a VAE; implemented classifier-free guidance; used advanced training strategies (EMA, improved noise scheduling) and an enhanced U-Net.

Results

Demonstrated compounding gains from latent-space operation and conditioning/scheduling improvements, achieving major FID reductions relative to baseline configurations.

Discussion

Clarifies where compute is spent (pixel vs latent), how sampling is accelerated (DDIM), and how conditioning/scheduling choices move the fidelity–speed frontier.

Role

Implemented core diffusion components and training loop; supported experimentation and analysis; documented the framework in a formal report.
