About Me

I am an M.S. mechanical engineering student at Carnegie Mellon University and a member of the BioSiMM Lab, where I study cardiovascular hemodynamics to better understand how disease alters flow, pressure, and transport. Alongside CFD, I build machine-learning analysis pipelines for extracting structure from simulation datasets, and I explore deep-learning approaches for accelerating solvers and high-dimensional modeling workflows.

I am especially interested in roles where simulation, numerical methods, and data analysis work together—whether the goal is patient-specific interpretation, safer infrastructure, or energy-efficient system design.

High-fidelity CFD (LES, DNS, multiphase, FSI) High-performance computing (OpenMP, MPI, profiling) Machine learning (diffusion, deep learning, CV, classical ML)

Project 1 · Research

Microvascular Resistance and Coronary Diagnostic Indices

BioSiMM Lab, Carnegie Mellon · 2024–present

Poster (PDF)

Objective: quantify how downstream microvascular conditions can shift coronary diagnostic indices across clinical thresholds—even when anatomy and lesion severity are held constant—so that interpretation is more patient-specific and clinically reliable.

Skills highlighted: Coronary CFD · Lumped-parameter networks · HPC automation · ParaView/VTK scripting · Python analysis

Details ↓ ▾

Introduction

Pressure- and flow-derived coronary indices can be sensitive to microvascular state. Improving interpretation can reduce misclassification risk and support better treatment decisions.

Methods

Built 3D coronary models coupled to lumped-parameter networks; executed parameter sweeps over resistance states; post-processed hemodynamics and indices at scale.

Results

Demonstrated that identical lesions can shift across decision thresholds under microvascular-only changes, motivating microvascular-aware interpretation.

Discussion

Highlights why “one-number” diagnostic thresholds should be contextualized by patient-specific downstream conditions, especially in borderline cases.

Role

Led simulation pipeline execution and post-processing automation; synthesized findings for communication in poster format.

Project 2 · Hemodynamics

Carotid Bifurcation Angle & Stenosis Influence on Hemodynamics

Carotid CFD Study · 2024

Video Report (PDF)

Objective: evaluate how geometric variation (bifurcation angle) and localized stenosis modulate WSS patterns linked to vascular risk, supporting better geometric intuition for disease progression and intervention planning.

Skills highlighted: Vascular CFD · Pulsatile flow · Mesh generation · Boundary conditions · WSS quantification

Details ↓ ▾

Introduction

Regions of abnormally low or high WSS are associated with plaque initiation and destabilization. Understanding how geometry drives these patterns supports better mechanistic reasoning.

Methods

Systematically varied angle and stenosis; ran pulsatile simulations; computed surface-area exposure to low/high WSS at key cardiac phases.

Results

Quantified changes in low-WSS exposure with stenosis/angle adjustments; summarized directional effects of geometry changes.

Discussion

Suggests geometry can amplify or mitigate hemodynamic risk factors, reinforcing the value of controlled parametric studies alongside patient-specific modeling.

Role

Built models/meshes, ran simulations, and performed WSS analysis; packaged outputs into report + media for clear review.

Project 3 · Environmental CFD

Decarbonization Building CFD: Reducing Re-Entrained Exhaust

Computational Fluid Dynamics · Fall 2024

Scalar transport (GIF)

Objective: reduce energy waste in direct-air-capture facility concepts by identifying shapes and operating conditions that minimize exhaust re-entrainment into inlets.

Skills highlighted: External-flow CFD · Transport modeling · Parametric studies · Engineering communication

Details ↓ ▾

Introduction

Re-entrainment can lower CO₂ removal efficiency by feeding processed exhaust back into the intake, increasing operating cost and reducing climate impact per unit energy.

Methods

Defined a simplified domain and boundary conditions; tested candidate geometries; tracked a passive scalar as a proxy for processed exhaust.

Results

Identified geometric/flow configurations associated with reduced near-inlet recirculation and lower exhaust fraction at the intake.

Discussion

Provides design guidance for early-stage facility layout decisions where small changes can materially impact efficiency and operating cost.

Role

Implemented transport diagnostics and contributed to simulation workflow + synthesis of results into a structured write-up.

Project 4 · Deep Learning

Modular Diffusion Model Framework for Image Synthesis (Built From Scratch)

Deep Learning · Fall 2024

Report (PDF)

Objective: implement a complete diffusion-based generative modeling stack from the ground up and quantify how architectural and algorithmic choices improve quality–compute tradeoffs in sampling and training.

Skills highlighted: PyTorch · DDPM/DDIM · Latent diffusion · Classifier-free guidance · Training stability (EMA/schedules) · Evaluation (FID/IS)

Details ↓ ▾

Introduction

Diffusion models produce high-quality samples but can be computationally intensive, especially in pixel space. This project targets practical efficiency improvements while preserving fidelity.

Methods

Implemented a baseline DDPM and expanded the framework to support accelerated deterministic sampling (DDIM), latent diffusion with a VAE, and classifier-free guidance for conditional generation. Added stability/quality strategies such as EMA, improved scheduling, and increased U-Net capacity.

Results

Compared variants using standard generative metrics and demonstrated that combining latent-space operation with guidance and training improvements yields substantial quality gains over baseline configurations.

Discussion

Clarifies the “design space” of diffusion systems: where compute is spent (pixel vs latent), how sampling is accelerated (DDIM), and how conditioning/scheduling choices shift the fidelity–speed frontier.

Role

Implemented core diffusion components and training loop; contributed experimentation and analysis, and documented the framework in a formal report.
